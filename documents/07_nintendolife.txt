Silksong is a large Metroidvania, so it is easy to get lost. Pharloom has multiple biomes
that hide secret paths and items. This guide gives a recommended route for each act,
focusing on the critical path toward the end of each act and the end of the game, while
noting where you can branch off to explore.


ACT 1 RECOMMENDED ROUTE

Recommended order for straight progression (not including optional Crests or Tools):
1. Moss Grotto
2. Bone Bottom
3. The Marrow
4. Deep Docks
5. Far Fields
6. Greymoor
7. Bellhart
8. Shellwood
9. Blasted Steps

You can enter Wormways early if you have a Simple Key, but it is much easier to explore
once you have the Cling Grip Ancestral Art, so do it after beating Sister Splinter at the
earliest.

You can reach Hunter's March as early as The Marrow, but you cannot progress through it
without the Drifter's Cloak, and the enemies and boss there are much easier once you have
Cling Grip.

Weavenest Atla is a very small area where you can upgrade your Hunter Crest, but it is
only accessible after defeating Widow, which you should do at the end of Shellwood (you
naturally loop back to Bellhart after getting Cling Grip).

The Slab is entirely optional at this point and is actually closer to The Citadel than the
rest of Pharloom. You may be captured by a bug in Pharloom, but you only have to explore
this jail area much later.

DECISION: Should I go through Blasted Steps or Sinner's Road?
Blasted Steps is the recommended way to reach the end of Act 1. It is west of Shellwood
and is much easier to explore than Sinner's Road. Sinner's Road is an alternate, harder
path that takes you through The Mist and the Exhaust Organ; you get a new Silk Skill for
defeating the boss inside the Organ, but The Mist is unpleasant. However, the boss at the
end of Blasted Steps is tricky and has a nasty run-back you will repeat often, so weigh
that trade-off.


ACT 2 RECOMMENDED ROUTE

In Act 2 the world opens up with more traversal tools and Ancestral Arts. Recommended
order around The Citadel for normal progression:
1. Choral Chambers
2. First Shrine / Songclave
3. Cogwork Core (bottom half)
4. Whispering Vaults (for the Vaultkeeper's Melody)
5. Underworks (for the Clawline Ancestral Art)
6. Mount Fay (for Double Jump / Faydown Cloak)
7. Cogwork Core (top half, for the Architect's Melody)
8. High Halls (for the Conductor's Melody)
9. The Cradle - Final Boss

Variant: if you entered the Grand Gate and started Act 2 from the Blasted Steps, you
automatically end up in the Underworks first; make your way through there before returning
to the Choral Chambers and heading to the First Shrine.

After you defeat the Cogwork Dancers (west of the First Shrine), you receive a quest to
acquire the Threefold Melody. As soon as you get the Vaultkeeper's Melody, go get the next
two traversal skills, Clawline and the Faydown Cloak (Double Jump); these two blow the rest
of the map wide open.

If you have not yet explored The Slab (because you were not captured in Act 1), do it on
the way to Mount Fay. After climbing the snowy mountain, Memorium, Sands of Karak, and
Wisp Thicket all become accessible. Whiteward is another optional area you can do whenever
(non-essential, though a Wish eventually takes you there).

DECISION: Which Threefold Melody should I get first?
Recommended order:
1. Vaultkeeper's Melody - needs no additional skills, so the Whispering Vaults is the
   easiest starting point (you must defeat a boss on the way).
2. Architect's Melody - needs at least Clawline to reach the top of the Cogwork Core;
   grabbing Double Jump first is also useful for the puzzle.
3. Conductor's Melody - the High Halls requires both Clawline and Double Jump, and this is
   put last because it may be one of the hardest challenges in the game: a ten-wave combat
   trial. Bring as many Mask Shards, Spool Fragments, and Needle Upgrades as you can.

Once you have all three melodies, talk to the trio of statues in the middle of the Cogwork
Core and play their songs; the elevator then opens to the final two bosses (for the normal
ending).

Bilewater is a totally optional area unless you are going for the true ending, so defeat
the final boss before going to Bilewater. For the True Ending and Act 3, see the separate
Act 3 unlock guide.


ACT 3 RECOMMENDED ROUTE

Act 3 starts with you compromised. Enemies are more dangerous, paths are blocked, and some
friends may no longer be friends.

After escaping The Cradle, you wake in Songclave with a new goal: free Pharloom from the
Void. Head to the Moss Grotto first; you must fight a boss before you can ride the Bell
Beast again. After Moss Grotto, head to The Abyss via Deep Docks, where you get an
important Silk Skill (Silk Soar). Back in Moss Grotto, you are given a new Needolin song and
a task to gather three Old Hearts. You can do these immediately, but it is highly
recommended to first max out health by collecting all Mask Shards, getting every Spool
Fragment, and fully upgrading your Needle. After getting the three Hearts, return to the
Shamans, head back to Deep Docks, re-enter the Void, and fight the final boss.

DECISION: What's the best order to collect the Old Hearts in?
You can get all three in any order; each is challenging in different ways and requires the
Elegy for the Deep song on the Needolin to access. Preferred order:
1. Heart of the Woods - by far the easiest, but the most hidden. Return to the collapsed
   lift shaft just west of the Underworks, use Silk Soar to find a broken wall on the left.
   Two bosses here, but easier than the other trials; the second is a gimmick boss, so
   bring long-range Tools.
2. Heart of Might - at the Coral Tower (Sands of Karak, where you fought the Vengeful
   Conchfly). Climb a battle tower and win three stages: 5 waves, then 10, then 15. A
   shortcut after the last phase leads to a shorter trial and a boss. The boss may be the
   easiest of the three, but the battle tower makes it harder to reach.
3. Heart of the Wild - held by one of the hardest bosses in the game, which is why it is
   recommended last. Access it through Hunter's March using Silk Soar by the statue of the
   Ant's leader, unlocking a new section of Far Fields. A few easy combat trials precede
   the boss; the boss is not easy.

Secret fourth Old Heart: if you freed the Green Prince in Sinner's Road, you gain access to
Verdania (a small area east of Greymoor). Defeat the boss there for the Conjoined Heart,
which can substitute for any one of the three Hearts. Even if you do not use it to access
the final boss, the fight is highly recommended.
