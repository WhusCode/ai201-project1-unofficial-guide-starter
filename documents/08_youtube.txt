ALL SKILLS / ABILITIES IN ACT I

Following this guide on a new playthrough leaves you able to fully explore every area
accessible before leaving Act 1 and starting Act 2. The ability order covered is:
Silkspear, fast travel (Bell Beast), Swift Step, Drifter's Cloak, Cling Grip, and Needolin.


BEGINNER BASICS (TUTORIAL TO BONE BOTTOM)

Bind to your pin and use basic abilities to reach the first bench. Explore a little for free
Shell Shards and Rosaries, the game's two currencies. Shell Shards are a lesser currency
used as donations for certain quests and to refill offensive tools found later. Rosaries are
the main currency for buying from vendors and are dropped on death; you reacquire them by
returning to where you died and striking the cocoon, but dying a second time before
recovering them loses them forever.

The first bench is in the Ruined Chapel. Benches restore health, manually save the game, and
set your respawn point. Just ahead is the first boss, the Moss Mother. Use Hornet's dive to
get extra attacks, and build silk with successful strikes; when the silk meter is full and
glowing, hold the input to restore up to 3 health. Getting struck mid-heal causes damage,
fails the heal, and loses the silk being used, so only heal when you have a few safe seconds.

After defeating the Moss Mother, go west and up the large bead rope, then east; a cutscene
and an NPC give you The Great Citadel objective, the primary Act 1 goal. Continue east into
Bone Bottom, the first town, and rest at the bench. Speak with the inhabitants (one is a
merchant), then head east into The Marrow.


MAPS AND THE SHAKRA NPC (THE MARROW)

Move through The Marrow going up and east toward the boss chamber. Several enemies spawn
there and must all be defeated to drop into a new chamber. Move west to unlock a gate back to
Bone Bottom, then east past an offering bowl that can be paid (Rosaries) to unlock a fancy
bench (optional). Continue east, then up and west, to a chamber with temporary platforms;
scale them to the top-west corner and follow the path toward an NPC singing. Hit a small
lever to open the way, grab free Rosaries and Shell Shards, and meet Shakra, who sells your
first maps and map-making supplies. Maps and the compass aren't strictly necessary but make
navigation much easier.

OPTIONAL EARLY ROSARY FARM (NEAR SHAKRA / MOSSHOME)
Up and west of Shakra is a path to the Mosshome zone. Move west along the elevated platform
past projectile-shooting bugs (their projectiles can be destroyed), hit a lever to open a
path below, drop in, and move east on the elevated section to a secluded chamber with a
bench; a lever here opens a shortcut back to Shakra. Farming loop: from this bench, move west
into Mosshome, defeat two easy enemies (5 Rosaries each), drop to the bottom floor for 3 more
enemies toward the bottom-east exit, then loop back to the bench. You get about 25 Rosaries
every 30 seconds. Equip the Magnetite Brooch if you have it.


SILKSPEAR (FIRST ABILITY)

From Shakra, move up and west into Moss Grotto, then to the top-west corner into a new
chamber. Move far east avoiding traps, then up and west to a weaver altar; inspecting it
unlocks the Silkspear. It costs silk to use and cuts through thick webbing to open new areas
(for example, a Frayed Rosary String to the west). Dropping down and moving east leads to
more webs; to the east is a bench, plus a Shaman NPC who gives the Berry Picking Wish
(optional side quest).


FAST TRAVEL (BELL BEAST BOSS, SILK HEART)

West of the Shaman, drop down and move west back toward a previous chamber, then to Shakra (a
bench here makes a closer spawn point for the boss). Free the Bell Beast with the Silkspear,
then defeat it. Its attacks deal 2 damage each but it has only 3 telegraphed moves, signaled
by the ground rumble before it emerges:
- Medium-height, wide plume: it charges (easiest time to deal damage).
- Wide but much taller plume: a leap attack; stay under it, wait for the landing, then strike.
- Narrow plume: it throws bouncing bells out to the sides (hard to punish; watch the bells).
Phase 2 is similar but adds an extra bell drop on each of the 3 major moves.

After the fight, interact with the floating orb, do a short platforming section, then a
second orb to Consume a Silk Heart (small regenerative silk capacity). Return to the Bellways
and jump up-right to unlock your first fast-travel post (to/from Bone Bottom). Strike the
lever in the next chamber east to unlock a bench and the path to a new region.


SWIFT STEP (THE MARROW PRISON TO DEEP DOCKS)

Start at the bench just east of The Marrow bellways. Move east through The Marrow (spike
pits, crumbling platforms). In the second horizontal chamber you can reach an upper area by
dive-attacking off bouncy red flowers, but the bottom route works; drop down a few levels and
follow the sign east into a jail with a free bench. From there go down and east into Deep
Docks (watch for spike pits, falling rocks, and lava). Follow Shakra's singing for the Deep
Docks Map (a sign-posted bench here costs Rosaries and is skippable).

To get Swift Step, head up and east through the major portion of Deep Docks. Exit at the
northeast corner (hit a lever to reach it). In the next chamber move east, jumping along hot
coal surfaces to avoid burn damage, then hit an explosive core to open the path. There is
another purchasable bench here worth considering before the upcoming enemy-wave encounter.
From that bench, go back west, then up and east to a narrow corridor; moving west reaches the
boss chamber with multiple enemy waves. Afterward, move west and drop into a new chamber,
watch for spike pits, strike the explosive rock at the southeast corner for a shortcut back,
then take the southwest exit and reach an altar; interact to unlock Swift Step.


DRIFTER'S CLOAK (FLEXIBLE SPINES QUEST)

From the Swift Step altar in Deep Docks, move west toward Shakra. (Optional, recommended:
follow the bench sign and drop into a nearby chamber, clear a boss room of enemy waves to
unlock a free bench near the next boss and a vendor selling the powerful Sting Shard tool.)
From the free bench, move up to the previous chamber and use Swift Step to open the boss
chamber to the east, where you fight Lace, a nimble foe that teaches the dodge function of
Swift Step. After Lace, move east and strike the lever to unlock a bench and gate.

Move east into Far Fields to the Bellways station (defeat humanoid foes along the way for
Rosaries if you have fewer than 30). Use the Sprint and air-dash functions of Swift Step to
stay on the upper path; exit top-east to the Far Fields Bellways and useful bench. (Optional
Far Fields Rosary farm: from the bench, go west, cross the top platforms past two explosive
clusters, drop and defeat the Skarr Scout west for 13 Rosaries, then Silkspear the paired
Skarr Scout and Skarr Stalker east for just over 50 Rosaries total; save and quit to return
to the bench and repeat.)

From the Far Fields Bellways, move west and drop down hugging the eastern wall to reach
Shakra for the Far Fields Map. Then move west, drop, and head east (watch the lava) through a
long chamber, then up to the Hut of the Seamstress (free bench). Speaking with the Seamstress
gives the Flexible Spines quest, required for the Drifter's Cloak. Move down and east to find
Hokers: strike one, avoid the spines it shoots, then strike the loose spines to harvest them.
You need 25 Spine Cores. Three Hokers are easily accessed here; leave and re-enter the
chamber to respawn them and repeat until you have 25. Return to the Seamstress's Hut and turn
them in to receive the Drifter's Cloak, which hovers in midair and rides updraft shafts found
throughout Pharloom.


CLING GRIP (FOURTH CHORUS BOSS, THEN SHELLWOOD AND SISTER SPLINTER)

From the Seamstress's Hut, move west back toward the Far Fields Bellways. The Fourth Chorus
boss interrupts the trip and is a chance to test the Drifter's Cloak: use it to avoid
horizontal swipes, and in the final phase ride the air streams on either side to hit the two
explosive cores, instantly ending the fight.

Go west, ride the air streams back to the Bellways bench, then ride a series of air streams
upward through the large vertical tube, striking explosive cores to open the way. At a closed
metal gate, follow the bench sign east (a paid structure with a vendor locks again on exit,
so buy everything on the first visit). Move east, loop up and west along the top to get above
the gate, hit the lever to open it, then ascend the tube past more cores and a lever, arriving
in Greymoor. East of here is Shakra (Greymoor map) and a free bench.

Move west through Greymoor toward the next zone. Past a cave of pilgrim-style enemies is the
Halfway Home (free bench); upstairs, speak with Nuu for the quest to defeat Pharloom's bugs
and the Hunter's Journal to track them. Continue west to a small arena (easy enemies), then
unlock shortcuts before the Moorwing boss, since you may run back several times. In the tower
structure, go up past scissor-wielding bugs and a spinning wheel, take the lower of two west
doors, descend and break a shortcut, drop down, hit a lever east for a major shortcut, then go
west and follow the town signs to the Moorwing.

MOORWING BOSS TIPS
A very difficult boss (took the guide author 10+ attempts). In phase two, all attacks get
substantial upgrades plus one new move:
- The flurry of leg attacks is NOT upgraded, so it remains a top opportunity to deal damage.
- A new move launches two blades simultaneously; stay under Moorwing and time jumps to also
  deal damage.
- The swoop attack is now followed by a single blade launch.
- The blade-launch attack now uses two blades; the second curves up earlier and circles back
  down to strike the ground. This is the most dangerous move, so get far away when telegraphed.
Note: this fight does not occur here if you completed the Flea questline and traveled with
them to arrive for the first time.

After Moorwing, move west into Haunted Bellhart, rest at the bench (note: quitting while
sitting here temporarily traps you in silk strands on reload). Move west into Shellwood,
ascend the large chamber in an S shape to the top-west exit, then drop west into a boss
chamber of enemy waves (the stickbugs are worst; store silk and hit them with Silkspears).
Go west and up to a purchasable bench (60 Rosaries) that serves as a checkpoint for two boss
fights. Down and west of the bench, ride a lift down to Shakra for the Bellhart and Shellwood
maps. Then go up the platforming section (nail-bounce off white flowers) to the top-east
exit, up and east through the next exit, then activate the lift near the top-east exit as a
shortcut back to the bench.

To reach Sister Splinter, ride the lift up, take the eastern exit, and platform to the
top-west exit into the boss chamber. Sister Splinter herself isn't too hard: destroy the
splinters she makes to keep room to maneuver and hit her. The danger is the adds she spawns,
best removed with Silkspears or ranged tools. After defeating her, move west, drop briefly,
then up to the top-west exit; in the next chamber move west, break vines, and needle-pogo up
to the top-east exit to reach the altar that unlocks Cling Grip (wall jump).


NEEDOLIN (WIDOW BOSS)

Start at the bench near Sister Splinter. Ride the elevator east up, or wall-jump up. Move
through the northeast passage, then all the way east; access a new area by wall-jumping up to
piles of bells and smacking through them. Move down and east past new enemy types, then west
and down; between two winged furms is a hidden breakable wall. Beyond it is a hole - jump over
it for now and move west through another hidden wall to open a shortcut back toward the bench
by breaking vines at the west end.

Heal at the bench if needed, return east through the last chamber, drop down a couple of holes
and move east to the Widow boss. Widow is comparable to Moorwing in difficulty; not all
attacks deal double damage but enough do, and it's a "death by a thousand cuts" fight. Phase
two is frantic at first but more predictable: Widow drops vertical or diagonal bells a few
times, then does a flurry attack; once you learn the rhythm, phase two gets easier. After
defeating Widow, plunge your needle and bind to unlock the Needolin. A short platforming
section exits a dream sequence; after waking, hit the nearby lever to unlock a bench and free
Bellhart town from its curse.

More mobility and traversal items exist, but only after starting Act 2. For now you can fully
explore Act 1; if you can't access an area, it's because it requires Act 2 progression.
