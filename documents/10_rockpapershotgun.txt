There are 53 Tools in the game. The best of them make Hornet far deadlier, and many provide
synergies when equipped together, opening up buildcrafting opportunities.


RED TOOLS (active, offensive)

Red Tools are active and offensive. Each costs its usage bar to activate; when the bar is
depleted the Tool becomes unusable until refreshed by spending Shell Shards at a resting
bench. With the Architect Crest, a specific input refreshes a Tool's bar on the go.

Sting Shard - Places a small spike trap in the air; when enemies touch it the spikes extend
and deal solid damage. A bit more expensive to use than some damaging Tools, but its
early-game availability via the merchant Forge Daughter makes it a strong pick. Littering an
arena with Sting Shards works well against bosses in close quarters.

Flea Brew - Increases your movement and attack speed for a short duration with no other
effects. One use vastly bumps up damage potential, especially with high-attack-speed Crests
like the Beast or Wanderer. The movement-speed boost is jarring at first but makes moving
around in a fight much easier once you adjust.

Delver's Drill - As long as you are above the enemy, one of the best burst-damage Tools in
the game, even more so with an upgraded Tool pouch. It shoots Hornet down at high speed and
drills into enemies up to five times on contact. Can kill most trash mobs in a single use
and makes tanky bosses less laborious.

Silkshot - Obtained from the shop near the top of Mount Fay; effectively a railgun. Fires a
laser beam that instantly reaches maximum range, ignores enemy armour, and pierces through
multiple foes. Hornet briefly hangs in the air when firing, so it doubles as a platforming
tool alongside its high damage.

Flintslate - "Superheats" your Needle, increasing its damage output by a full 50% for a
brief duration. Like Binding and the Silkshot, using it midair makes Hornet hang, which is
useful for avoiding a collision with an enemy on your descent.


BLUE TOOLS (passive buffs)

Blue Tools provide passive buffs and bonuses when equipped, and can change or improve how you
use Silk, particularly the damage and effects of Silk Skills. There are no drawbacks, but no
Crest has more than three Blue slots, so you can never equip all of them at once.

Injector Band - Makes Binding to heal take less time. The faster heal saves you from wasting
Silk by getting hit mid-Bind, which happens at least a few times per playthrough.

Multibinder - Splits the Binding heal animation into two parts, each Bind healing two Masks,
for four health restored total. Binding takes almost twice as long; mitigate this by pairing
it with the Injector Band, whose effects complement it.

Volt Filament - Adds a pink lightning effect that drastically improves the damage of all Silk
Skills, plus a brief minor damage-over-time effect. Valuable in the later game as enemy
health pools grow. (Obtained by killing the big shocking enemy near the Sands of Karak path -
i.e. Voltvyrm.)

Claw Mirrors - A Binding-centric Tool (single and dual versions). When you Bind to heal, it
causes a sparkling explosion around Hornet that damages nearby enemies. Works with the
Multibinder (two explosions in quick succession). With the Warding Bell equipped, that Tool's
damage-mitigation effect makes the Mirror explosion much larger.

Longclaw - Extends the range of your Needle regardless of equipped Crest, helping with
attacks that fall just short. Especially appreciated on short-range Crests like the Wanderer,
but longer ones like the active Beast Crest benefit too. The extra range lets you keep tough
bosses at arm's length while still dealing damage. (Reward for completing the Broodfeast quest
for the Huntress.)


YELLOW TOOLS (utility and quality-of-life)

Yellow Tools are a catch-all: some give buffs and bonuses, others offer quality-of-life
exploration features, and a select few have powerful battle-turning effects.

Compass - Shows Hornet's current location on the map (provided the area map is unlocked), and
her icon moves with her as she explores with the map open. Not strictly necessary, but saves
a few seconds whenever you'd otherwise guess your position in Pharloom.

Weighted Belt - Greatly reduces knockback from enemy attacks (some knockback remains). Helps
Hornet withstand hits more efficiently and avoid being pushed into hazards, letting you stand
your ground or keep up an offensive without bouncing around the arena.

Magnetite Dice - Gives a small chance to negate a single hit entirely (no damage), though you
still take the knockback and gain the invincibility frames. Pairs reasonably with the
Weighted Belt. Luck-based, so don't rely on it, but it can save an otherwise lost fight.

Ascendant's Grip - After getting the Cling Grip ability, this Tool lets you stick to walls
without sliding down, so you can line up your next jump more carefully. Makes trickier
platforming sections much less frustrating.
